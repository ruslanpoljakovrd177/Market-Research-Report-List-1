<p><p>The feed carbohydrate market is divided into four types: glucose, sucrose, starch, and cellulose. Glucose is a simple sugar that is easily absorbed by animals and used as a quick energy source. Sucrose, also known as table sugar, provides energy but requires enzymes to break it down before absorption. Starch is a complex carbohydrate found in grains and vegetables, providing sustained energy release. Cellulose, a major component of plant cell walls, cannot be digested by animals but contributes to their fiber intake, aiding in digestion.</p></p>
