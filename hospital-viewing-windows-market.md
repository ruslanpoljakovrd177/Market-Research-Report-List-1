<p><p>There are two main types of hospital viewing windows: fixed and sliding. Fixed windows are immovable and securely fastened to the surrounding structure. They offer a more permanent and stable option for observing patients or medical procedures. On the other hand, sliding windows can be opened or closed, providing flexibility to control the flow of communication and access to patients. These windows are often used in healthcare settings where temporary barriers or varying levels of privacy are necessary. Both types serve the purpose of allowing observation while maintaining patient confidentiality and safety.</p></p>
