<p><p>The bridge design and construction market consists of various types of bridges. Beam bridges are simple and commonly used, consisting of horizontal beams supported by piers or abutments. Rigid frame bridges are stiffer and can span longer distances efficiently, using supporting columns and beams. Cable-stayed bridges employ cables connected to towers that support the bridge deck, offering flexibility and elegant designs. Suspension bridges use cables suspended from towers, providing high strength and long spans. Arch bridges have a curved shape, distributing weight along its curve and are known for their aesthetically pleasing appearance.</p></p>
