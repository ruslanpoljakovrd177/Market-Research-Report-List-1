The black type glue is manufactured by heating animal bones, resulting in a dark-colored and strong adhesive. On the other hand, the amber type glue is made by extracting proteins from animal hides, resulting in a light golden color and a flexible adhesive. Both types of glue are widely used in various industries such as woodworking, bookbinding, and handicrafts.</p></p>
