<p><p>Livestock external medicine refers to medication primarily applied on the outer surface of an animal's body, such as sprays, creams, or ointments. This type of medicine is often used for treating skin disorders, wounds, or external parasites. On the other hand, livestock internal medicine refers to medications administered internally, typically through injections, tablets, or liquids. These medicines address internal health issues, including respiratory and digestive disorders, infections, or nutritional deficiencies. Both external and internal medicine play crucial roles in ensuring the overall health and well-being of livestock animals.</p></p>
