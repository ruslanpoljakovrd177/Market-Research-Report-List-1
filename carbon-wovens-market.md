<p><p>The Carbon Wovens market consists of two types of weaves: General Weave and Twill Weave. The General Weave is a simple and common pattern where carbon fibers are interlaced in a regular crisscross manner, resulting in a symmetric appearance. It is commonly used in applications requiring balanced strength and stiffness. On the other hand, Twill Weave is a more complex pattern where the carbon fibers are interlaced in a diagonal pattern, creating a distinct and decorative V-shaped appearance. Twill Weave offers improved drapability and conformability, making it suitable for applications that require flexibility and style.</p></p>
