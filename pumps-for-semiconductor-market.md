<p><p>Pumps for the semiconductor market can be classified into three main types: dry pumps, molecular pumps, and other pumps. Dry pumps are designed to handle corrosive gases and operate without any oil lubrication, ensuring a clean and contaminant-free environment. Molecular pumps, on the other hand, use the momentum transfer principle to create a vacuum, making them ideal for high-vacuum applications. Lastly, the "other" category includes various types of pumps such as rotary vane pumps, scroll pumps, and turbomolecular pumps, each offering unique features and capabilities for specific semiconductor manufacturing processes.</p></p>
