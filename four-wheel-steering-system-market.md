<p><p>The four-wheel steering system market encompasses various types, including crab steering, passive rear wheel steering, and articulated steering. Crab steering allows all four wheels to turn in the same direction, enabling the vehicle to move diagonally. Passive rear wheel steering enables the back wheels to turn slightly in the opposite direction of the front wheels, resulting in improved stability and maneuverability at high speeds. Articulated steering involves two parts of a vehicle being connected by a joint, enabling enhanced maneuverability and flexibility in challenging terrains.</p></p>
