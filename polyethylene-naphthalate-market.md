<p><ul><li>Ester process</li><li>Acid process</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Polyethylene Naphthalate (PEN) is produced through two different processes, namely the Ester process and the Acid process, which play a crucial role in the market. The Ester process involves the condensation of ethylene glycol and naphthalene dicarboxylic acid, resulting in high-performance PEN suitable for applications requiring excellent thermal and mechanical properties. On the other hand, the Acid process utilizes terephthalic acid and ethylene glycol, producing cost-effective PEN used mainly in packaging films. The market for these different types of PEN is determined by industry-specific requirements and cost considerations.</p></p>
