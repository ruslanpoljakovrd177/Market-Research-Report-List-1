<p><p>Turboprop engines are classified into two main market types: axial-flow type and centrifugal type. Axial-flow type turboprop engines use a system of axial compressors to increase the pressure of the incoming air before it passes through a combustion chamber. This type offers high efficiency and power output. On the other hand, centrifugal type turboprop engines use centrifugal compressors to compress the air, providing a simpler and more compact design. While less efficient, these engines offer better reliability and lower maintenance costs. Both types are used in different applications within the aviation industry.</p></p>
