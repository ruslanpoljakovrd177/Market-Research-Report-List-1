<p><p>Injectable nanomedicines are innovative therapeutic agents that are administered via intravenous or intramuscular routes. They can be classified into several types based on their structures and properties. Liposomes are lipid-based nanoparticles with a hollow core for drug encapsulation. Micelles consist of amphiphilic molecules arranged in a spherical shape for solubilizing hydrophobic drugs. Nanocrystals are crystalline nanoparticles with a high surface area for enhancing drug dissolution. Polymeric nanoparticles are composed of biocompatible polymers used for drug delivery. Lastly, there are other diverse types of injectable nanomedicines developed using innovative materials and delivery systems. These nanomedicines offer promising solutions for delivering drugs effectively and efficiently to targeted sites in the body.</p></p>
