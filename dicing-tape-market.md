<p><p>Dicing tape is a type of pressure-sensitive adhesive tape used in the semiconductor industry for the dicing or separation of integrated circuit (IC) chips. It is typically made of a polyester film with a layer of pressure-sensitive adhesive on one side. The adhesive side of the tape is applied to the surface of the wafer, and then the wafer is diced into individual chips using a dicing saw or laser cutter. The tape helps to hold the chips in place during the dicing process and provides protection to prevent damage or breaking.</p><p>The dicing tape market is expected to witness substantial growth in the coming years. <p><p>The dicing tape market can be classified into two types: UV curable type and non-UV type. UV curable dicing tapes are designed to be cured using ultraviolet light, providing excellent adhesion during the dicing process. On the other hand, non-UV dicing tapes do not require UV light for curing and offer good adhesion through alternative methods. Both types serve the purpose of supporting and protecting the semiconductor wafer during the dicing process, with UV curable tapes offering additional benefits of quick curing and high bond strength.</p></p>
