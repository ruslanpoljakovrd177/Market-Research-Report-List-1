<p><p>A step-down voltage regulator is a device used to decrease the voltage level from a higher value to a lower value. There are two main types of step-down voltage regulators in the market: AC (alternating current) step-down voltage regulator and DC (direct current) step-down voltage regulator. AC step-down voltage regulators are designed to convert high AC voltages to lower, more manageable levels, whereas DC step-down voltage regulators are specifically built to reduce DC voltages. Both types play a crucial role in various electronic devices and power supply systems.</p></p>
