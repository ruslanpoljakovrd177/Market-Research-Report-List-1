<p><p>The robots in the food and beverage industry can be classified into three types based on their payload capacity. Low payload robots have a capacity of less than 10 kg and are typically used for tasks that require precision and flexibility, such as picking, placing, and packaging small food items. Medium payload robots, with a capacity of 10-100 kg, are suitable for tasks like palletizing, depalletizing, or handling larger food products. High payload robots, with a capacity exceeding 100 kg, are primarily used for heavy-duty tasks such as loading and unloading heavy containers or equipment in the food and beverage industry.</p></p>
