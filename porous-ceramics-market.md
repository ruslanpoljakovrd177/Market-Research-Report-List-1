<p><p>Porous ceramics are categorized into two main types: oxides ceramics and non-oxides ceramics. Oxides ceramics are composed of metallic and non-metallic elements, such as alumina and zirconia, and are widely used due to their high thermal and chemical stability. Non-oxides ceramics, on the other hand, are made up of materials like silicon carbide and boron nitride, offering exceptional mechanical properties and heat resistance. Both types find extensive applications in industries like aerospace, automotive, and healthcare due to their unique porous structure, which allows for controlled fluid flow and filtration capabilities.</p></p>
