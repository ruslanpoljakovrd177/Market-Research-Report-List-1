<p><p>TIRF (Total Internal Reflection Fluorescence) microscopes are used to study biological samples with high resolution. There are two market types based on the method of TIRF generation. The first is the Prism Method, where a glass prism is used to create the critical angle needed for TIRF. The second is the Objective Lens Method, which utilizes a high numerical aperture objective lens to achieve TIRF. Both methods allow selective illumination of thin sample layers near the coverslip, enabling researchers to observe cellular processes with minimal background noise.</p></p>
