<p><p>Connector protection devices, such as Type I and Type II, are devices designed to safeguard connectors from damage or failure caused by excessive mechanical stress, electrical surges, or environmental factors. </p><p>Type I protectors are typically used to shield against overvoltage and transient voltage events, such as lightning strikes, and provide high-level protection for connector pins. These devices are commonly employed in outdoor applications where electrical systems are exposed to the elements.</p><p>Type II protectors, on the other hand, offer a lower level of protection against overvoltage events and can handle surges originating from within the system. They are commonly used in indoor applications to shield connectors from power fluctuations and equipment switching transients.</p><p>Both types of connector protection devices play a crucial role in ensuring the integrity and functionality of electrical systems by preventing damage to connectors and the equipment they serve.</p></p>
