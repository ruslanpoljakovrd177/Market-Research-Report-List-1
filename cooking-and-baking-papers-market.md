<p><p>Cooking and baking papers, also known as parchment paper or baking parchment, are thin sheets of paper coated with a non-stick silicone coating. They are commonly used in various food preparation and cooking processes such as baking, steaming, roasting, and grilling. Cooking and baking papers are primarily used to prevent food from sticking to baking pans or trays, making it easier to remove the cooked food without any residue. They also help in reducing the need for greasing the pans with oil or butter.</p><p>The current outlook of the cooking and baking papers market is promising. The market is driven by several factors such as the growing awareness among consumers regarding healthier cooking methods and the increasing demand for convenience food products. The use of cooking and baking papers is also being driven by the growing trend of home baking and DIY cooking, especially among the millennial population.</p><p>Additionally, the rising preference for sustainable and eco-friendly products is expected to boost the demand for cooking and baking papers. These papers are biodegradable and can be easily disposed of, making them an environmentally friendly option compared to aluminum foil or plastic wraps. Manufacturers are also focusing on developing innovative products with additional features such as non-stick coatings on both sides, which further enhances the convenience of using cooking and baking papers.</p><p>The future of the cooking and baking papers market looks promising with steady growth expected. The market is projected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 10.7% during the forecast period. The increasing penetration of e-commerce and online grocery platforms is anticipated to further drive the market growth. Moreover, the ongoing advancements in technology and the introduction of new cooking and baking paper variants are likely to create lucrative opportunities for market players. However, the market is also facing challenges such as the availability of various substitute products and the high competition from aluminum foil and silicone baking mats. Nonetheless, the growing consumer preference for healthier cooking practices and the convenience provided by cooking and baking papers are expected to sustain the market growth in the coming years.</p></p>
