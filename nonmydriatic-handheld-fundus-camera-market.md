<p><p>The nonmydriatic handheld fundus camera market comprises two main types: the optical system and the image acquisition system. The optical system refers to the mechanism that captures the fundus image by using various optical components such as lenses and mirrors. On the other hand, the image acquisition system encompasses the technology utilized to capture and store the fundus image. These components work together to provide high-quality images of the interior surface of the eye without the need for pupil dilation, resulting in more efficient and comfortable eye examinations.</p></p>
