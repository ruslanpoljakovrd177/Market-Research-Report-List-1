<p><p>The living insecticide market includes three types: microbial pesticides, plant pesticides, and biochemical pesticides. Microbial pesticides consist of living microorganisms that kill or inhibit pests. Plant pesticides are derived from naturally occurring substances in plants, such as neem oil. Biochemical pesticides are synthetic versions of naturally occurring substances, like insect sex pheromones, that disrupt pests' mating behaviors. All three types of living insecticides provide effective and environmentally friendly alternatives to traditional chemical-based pesticides, as they target pests specifically while minimizing harm to humans and the surrounding ecosystem.</p></p>
