<p><p>Syngas, or synthetic gas, is produced through different methods, primarily including natural gas reforming, naphtha reforming, coal gasification, and other alternative techniques. Natural gas reforming involves converting methane into syngas, while naphtha reforming involves the transformation of naphtha feedstock. Coal gasification utilizes coal as the raw material to generate syngas through a chemical reaction. Other techniques encompass various biomass feedstocks, municipal waste, and industrial byproducts. These different market types represent the diverse ways in which syngas can be produced, offering flexibility and wide-ranging options for its applications in industries such as chemicals, power generation, and transportation.</p></p>
