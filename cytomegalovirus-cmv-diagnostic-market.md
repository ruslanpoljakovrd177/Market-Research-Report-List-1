<p><p>The Cytomegalovirus (CMV) Diagnostic Market includes different types of tests used to detect the presence of CMV in a patient's blood or urine samples. Blood-based tests, such as serological tests, detect CMV-specific antibodies or antigens in the blood, indicating a previous or active infection. Urine-based tests, like polymerase chain reaction (PCR), detect the presence of CMV DNA in urine samples, helping diagnose CMV infections in newborns or immunocompromised individuals. Both blood and urine tests are essential in diagnosing and monitoring CMV infections to provide appropriate treatment and prevent complications.</p></p>
