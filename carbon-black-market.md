<p><ul><li>Thermal Black</li><li>Furnace Black</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The carbon black market consists of two main types, namely thermal black and furnace black. Thermal black is produced by the partial combustion of natural gases, resulting in a fine black powder that is primarily used in the rubber industry to enhance the durability of tires. On the other hand, furnace black is manufactured by burning heavy aromatic oils with controlled combustion, resulting in a high-yield carbon black that finds its applications in the manufacturing of rubber goods, printing inks, and plastics. Both thermal black and furnace black contribute significantly to the carbon black market, serving various industries with their distinct properties and functionalities.</p></p>
