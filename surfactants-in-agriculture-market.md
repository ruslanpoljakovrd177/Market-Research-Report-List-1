<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Surfactants in agriculture are classified into four main types: non-ionic, anionic, cationic, and amphoteric. Non-ionic surfactants do not have an electrical charge and are commonly used in pesticide formulations to enhance the spreading and adherence of chemicals on plants. Anionic surfactants carry a negative charge and are effective for controlling pests that have a waxy cuticle. Cationic surfactants have a positive charge and are typically used in fertilizers and nutrient solutions to improve nutrient uptake by plant roots. Amphoteric surfactants can function as both anionic and cationic surfactants, offering versatility in agricultural applications.</p></p>
