<p><p>The marine hybrid propulsion market consists of three main types: diesel-electric, parallel hybrid, and serial hybrid systems. Diesel-electric propulsion uses a diesel engine to generate electricity, which then powers an electric motor to drive the propellers. Parallel hybrid systems combine a traditional diesel engine with an electric motor that can work together or independently. Serial hybrid systems rely primarily on electric power, with a smaller diesel engine used solely to charge the batteries. These technologies provide varying levels of efficiency and environmental benefits for marine vessels.</p></p>
