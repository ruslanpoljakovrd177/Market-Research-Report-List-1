<p><p>The acrylic fibre market consists of three main types: staple fibre, tows, and tops. Staple fibres are short lengths of acrylic fibre, typically around 2-5 inches long, that can be spun into yarns or fabrics. Tows are continuous strands of acrylic fibre, consisting of multiple filaments grouped together, often used for making fabrics and textiles. Tops are combed and parallelized acrylic fibre slivers, which are further processed to create higher quality yarns. Each type serves different manufacturing purposes within the acrylic fibre market.</p></p>
