Secondly, Macroalgae, also known as seaweed, is a larger type of algae that has high levels of essential nutrients and can be used in food products, fertilizers, and even biofuels. Cyanobacteria, commonly known as blue-green algae, are another type that has potential uses in bioplastics, wastewater treatment, and nutraceuticals. Lastly, other types of algal biomass include red algae and brown algae, which also have varied applications in various industries.</p></p>
