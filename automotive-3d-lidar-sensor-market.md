<p><ul><li>Mechanical Lidar</li><li>Solid State Lidar</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive 3D LiDAR sensor market consists of two main types: mechanical LiDAR and solid-state LiDAR. Mechanical LiDAR sensors use moving parts such as rotating mirrors or prisms to scan their environment, providing accurate ranging and high resolution. However, they are bulky, expensive, and prone to wear and tear. On the other hand, solid-state LiDAR sensors use non-moving parts like semiconductor components to emit and receive laser pulses. They are smaller, more affordable, and more durable than mechanical LiDAR sensors, making them increasingly popular in the automotive industry.</p></p>
