<p><p>Deep Ultraviolet (DUV) photoresist refers to a special type of resist material used in the semiconductor industry for the fabrication of microprocessors and other integrated circuits. DUV photoresists are primarily used in the photolithography process to transfer circuit patterns onto silicon wafers during the semiconductor manufacturing process. They are specifically designed to be sensitive to DUV light, typically with a wavelength of around 248 nm or 193 nm, enabling high-resolution patterning on the wafer.</p><p>The deep ultraviolet (DUV) photoresist market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 10.4% during the forecast period. Several factors are driving this growth. Firstly, the increasing demand for advanced electronic devices, such as smartphones, tablets, and IoT devices, is boosting the demand for semiconductors, thereby driving the demand for DUV photoresists. Additionally, the growing trend of miniaturization and increasing complexity of semiconductor devices is further fueling the market growth.</p><p>Moreover, advancements in semiconductor fabrication technologies, including the development of advanced lithography techniques such as immersion lithography and extreme ultraviolet (EUV) lithography, are also contributing to the market growth. These advanced technologies require high-performance DUV photoresists to achieve high-resolution patterning.</p><p>Furthermore, ongoing research and development activities to improve the performance of DUV photoresists, such as enhancing their sensitivity, resolution, and etching resistance, are expected to open up new growth opportunities in the market. Additionally, the increasing focus on developing eco-friendly and cost-effective DUV photoresist materials is also anticipated to drive market growth in the coming years.</p><p>Overall, the deep ultraviolet (DUV) photoresist market is forecasted to witness significant growth due to the increasing demand for advanced electronic devices and ongoing technological advancements in the semiconductor industry.</p></p>
