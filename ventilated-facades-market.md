<p><p>A ventilated facade, also known as a rainscreen or curtain wall system, is an exterior cladding system that allows air to circulate between the building envelope and the cladding, providing numerous benefits such as improved thermal performance, moisture control, and aesthetic appeal. It consists of an outer layer (cladding) that is separated from the building structure by an air cavity, creating a ventilated space.</p><p>The ventilated facades market is witnessing steady growth and is expected to continue expanding at a CAGR of 5.8% during the forecast period. Increasing demand for energy-efficient buildings, growing awareness regarding sustainable construction materials, and rising urbanization are some of the key factors driving market growth.</p><p>In terms of market analysis, the ventilated facades market is segmented based on material type, system type, application, and region. Materials used for ventilated facades include glass, metal, ceramic, composite, and others. Among these, the glass segment holds a significant share due to its aesthetic appeal and durability. The demand for metal cladding systems is also expected to rise due to their lightweight nature and ease of installation.</p><p>Based on the system type, the market is categorized into drained cavity and pressure-equalized systems. The drained cavity system is more commonly used as it provides better moisture management and prevents water from penetrating behind the cladding. This system is highly suitable for high-rise buildings.</p><p>On the basis of application, the market is segmented into commercial, residential, and industrial sectors. The commercial sector dominates the market due to the increasing number of office buildings, shopping malls, and hotels. The residential sector is also witnessing substantial growth, driven by urbanization and the need for aesthetically pleasing and energy-efficient homes.</p><p>In conclusion, the ventilated facades market is expected to register significant growth in the coming years. Factors such as energy efficiency requirements, sustainable construction practices, and urbanization are driving the demand for ventilated facade systems. The market is characterized by the use of materials such as glass and metal, with the commercial sector being the major end-user.</p></p>
