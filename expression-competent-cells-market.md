<p><p>Competent cells, mainly used in molecular biology research, refer to host cells that can easily take up foreign DNA molecules. The competent cells market consists of two major types: chemically competent cells and electrocompetent cells. Chemically competent cells are treated with chemicals to increase their membrane permeability and allow easy DNA uptake. On the other hand, electrocompetent cells are made competent using an electric pulse to create temporary pores in the cell membrane, enabling DNA entry. These different types cater to various scientific needs in genetic engineering and gene expression studies.</p></p>
