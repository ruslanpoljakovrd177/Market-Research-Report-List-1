<p><p>Electrophoresis buffers are essential for carrying out gel electrophoresis, a technique used to separate DNA, RNA, and proteins based on their size and charge. TBE buffer (Tris-borate-EDTA) and TAE buffer (Tris-acetate-EDTA) are two widely used buffer types. TBE provides better resolution for larger DNA fragments, while TAE is commonly used for smaller fragments. Other buffer types include Tricine-EDTA, MOPS-EDTA, and MES-EDTA, which offer specific advantages depending on the experimental requirements. The choice of buffer type depends on factors such as the size of the molecules being separated and the desired resolution of the experiment.</p></p>
