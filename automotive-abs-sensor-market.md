<p><p>An Automotive ABS (Anti-lock Braking System) Sensor is a crucial component in modern vehicles that helps prevent wheel lock-up during braking, thereby improving steering control and reducing stopping distances. It works by constantly monitoring the rotational speed of each wheel and transmitting this information to the vehicle's braking system. If the sensor detects that a wheel is about to lock up, it signals the system to adjust the braking force on that wheel, allowing it to continue rotating and preventing skidding.</p><p>The current outlook of the Automotive ABS Sensor Market is promising, with a steady growth rate expected during the forecasted period. <p><p>The Automotive ABS Sensor market consists of two main types: Hall Type and Magnetic Electric Type. </p><p>Hall Type ABS sensors use a Hall effect sensor to measure the rotation of the wheel and detect the speed and direction of the vehicle. They are highly accurate and widely used in modern vehicles.</p><p>On the other hand, Magnetic Electric Type ABS sensors utilize a magnetic field to detect the wheel speed and rotation. They are cost-effective and commonly found in older vehicles.</p><p>Both types of sensors play a crucial role in providing essential information to the ABS system for effective brake control and anti-locking functionality in automobiles.</p></p>
