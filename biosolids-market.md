<p><p>The biosolids market can be categorized into two main types: industrial wastewater and municipal wastewater. Industrial wastewater refers to the by-products and residual materials generated from industrial processes, which are often rich in nutrients and organic matter. These materials can be treated and transformed into biosolids for beneficial use in agriculture and land reclamation. On the other hand, municipal wastewater reflects the waste material generated from households, businesses, and institutions, which can also be processed into biosolids for similar purposes. Both markets involve the treatment and utilization of waste materials, promoting sustainable waste management practices.</p></p>
